The Edgar Thomson Steel Works

Built in 1874 by Alexander Lyman Holley, Edgar Thomson Steel Works is a steel mill that uses combines raw material in blast furnaces to produce liquid iron, which is then refined to create steel. As of 2004, there are two blast furnaces still in operation.

The steel mill was named after John Edgar Thomson who was the President of the Pennsylvania Railroad.
